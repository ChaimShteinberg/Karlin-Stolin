The Birth of a Spiritual Movement

Introduction

In the mid-18th century, a powerful spiritual revolution began to spread across Eastern Europe. Known as Hasidism, this movement brought a new focus on heartfelt prayer, joy, and deep connection with God. One of the first and most influential branches of Hasidism was founded in the town of Karlin, in present-day Belarus.

Rabbi Aharon the Great of Karlin

The Karlin Hasidic dynasty was established by Rabbi Aharon ben Jacob of Karlin (1736–1772), known as Aharon HaGadol. He was a student of the Maggid of Mezeritch and was deeply influenced by the Baal Shem Tov. His prayers were fiery and full of emotion, and he attracted followers who wanted to serve God with intensity and awe.
