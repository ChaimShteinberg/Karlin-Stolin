The Masters of the Karlin Dynasty

Great Leaders

After Rabbi Aharon’s passing, leadership passed to Rabbi Shlomo of Karlin (1740–1792), known for his deep faith and tragic death at the hands of Russian soldiers. He taught that even the simplest Jew could connect to God with sincerity.

Later leaders included:

  1. Rabbi Asher of Stolin (d. 1826), son of Rabbi Aharon the Second
  2. Rabbi Yochanan Perlow (d. 1873), a great unifier
  3. Rabbi Baruch Meir Yaakov Shochet, the current Rebbe

These figures led the movement through war, exile, and renewal. Each added their own light to the fire of Karlin.
